  【击鼓传花】


击鼓传花是中国古代传统民间酒宴上的助兴游戏，属于酒令的一种，又称“击鼓催花”，在唐代时就已出现。国外也有类似的小游戏，叫做Pass the parcel，但该游戏的目是获得礼物。


Ji gu chuan hua is a kind of Chinese traditional drinking game appeared in Tang dynasty popular among folk parties. It is also known as “beating the drum”. There is a similar game abroad, called Pass the parcel, but its purpose is for a gift.

数人围成圆圈坐下，其中一人拿花（或一小物件）；一人蒙眼击鼓（或其他能发出声音的物体），鼓响时众人开始依次传花，至鼓停止为止。此时花在谁手中，谁表演节目（古代往往是作诗）。


Several people sit in a circle, and one of them takes a flower (or other items). One man with eyes covered by a cloth beats a drum (or other subjects that could make sounds). When the drum is sounded, people begin to pass the flowers in turn. At the moment the sound is stopped, and the person who takes the flower has to perform. Writing a poem is quiet popular at ancient time.
